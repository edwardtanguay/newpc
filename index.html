🎯 Core Requirements

What the new machine absolutely needs to do:

  • Virtualization: Run numerous Docker containers
  • OS Integration: Windows + Linux (WSL2) running simultaneously
  • Creative: Video editing with OBS Studio
  • Use Case: Heavy software development, No Gaming
  • I/O: Single external monitor support
  • Mobility: Not an important factor. The laptop can be larger/bulkier to save money, and battery life only needs to be at least 4 hours.

⚙️ Hardware Analysis

Based on your requirements, here is an analysis of what you think you need, and what you actually need for modern dev workflows:

Storage: At least 1 TB SSD

✅ Correct, but consider 2 TB. Docker images, WSL virtual disks, and video files from OBS will consume 1TB very quickly. Ensure the laptop has a fast Gen 4 NVMe SSD.

RAM: More than 16 GB

❌ Correction: You absolutely need 32 GB, and ideally 64 GB. Running WSL2, Docker, and an IDE simultaneously will devour 16GB. 32 GB is the absolute minimum for your use case.

GPU: A fast graphics card

✅ Correct, but specifically you need an NVIDIA GPU. For OBS hardware encoding (NVENC), NVIDIA dominates. You don't need a top-tier gaming GPU, but you do need VRAM. Look for an RTX 4060 (8GB VRAM) or RTX 4070 (8GB VRAM).

Monitors: 1 External Display + Large Laptop Screen

💡 Concept to understand: By choosing a laptop with a massive 17-inch or 18-inch display, you gain desktop-like screen real estate on the go. Any modern laptop will effortlessly power your single additional external monitor via HDMI or USB-C.

📉 Cost Reduction

If you need to reduce the price, here is what you can compromise on:

  • Buy a smaller SSD (1TB) and upgrade later: Laptop manufacturers often overcharge for storage, typically demanding €150 to €250 extra to upgrade from 1TB to 2TB at checkout. However, buying a high-quality 1TB NVMe SSD yourself only costs around €60 to €90. Many Windows laptops allow you to easily add a second SSD later when you need more space, saving you a lot of money. (Just verify the model supports a second drive!)
  • Opt for an RTX 4060 instead of 4070/4080: The "RTX 4060" refers to the graphics card (GPU) made by NVIDIA. Think of it as a specialized processor for visuals. The 4070 and 4080 are much faster (and vastly more expensive) versions designed primarily for high-end gaming. Since you aren't gaming, the mid-range RTX 4060 provides all the necessary hardware features (like 8GB of video memory) needed for your OBS video encoding without the massive price markup of the higher tiers.
  • Skip the 4K / OLED screen: Even with a large 17-inch or 18-inch laptop display, a high-quality standard IPS panel (QHD/1600p) is excellent for development and will save you hundreds of Euros compared to OLED options.
